About Us

Who We Are

Flowers On Thorns Community-Based Organization (CBO), located in Roysambu, Nairobi County, Kenya, is dedicated to transforming the lives of vulnerable youth and young children in Roysambu and beyond. Through holistic interventions—ranging from psychosocial support, trauma recovery, and vocational training to education, life skills development, and advocacy—FOT targets marginalized young people, especially young mothers, street-involved youth, and those affected by early pregnancy, addiction, and crime.

Our approach includes running rehabilitation and daycare centers, distributing care packages, nurturing talents, and offering safe shelter and reintegration services.

We work in partnership with communities, institutions, and local government using participatory methods to ensure sustainability and long-term impact, with the ultimate goal of building a safer, more empowered, and self-reliant youth population. This strategic plan will be implemented through participatory approaches with both public and private partners, locally and internationally.

Our History

Flowers On Thorns began in 2015 as Juakali Young Moms Self-Help Group, focusing solely on supporting young mothers. In 2019, after recognizing the challenges faced by boys and young men, the group expanded to include them and rebranded as Flowers On Thorns. The organization was officially registered as a Community-Based Organization (CBO) on 19th November 2024.

Our Mission

To build a sustainable future for the youth through psychosocial support, education, and life skills training.

Our Vision

A thriving community with productive, independent, and transformed youth.

Core Values

1. Christ-centered – Guided by Christian principles in all we do.
2. Excellence – Striving for the highest standards in service delivery.
3. Integrity, Accountability & Transparency – Building trust with honesty and openness.
4. Teamwork – Working together with unity and shared purpose.

Our Goals

• To rehabilitate and empower street and vulnerable youth.
• To provide skills training and economic opportunities for young mothers and youth.
• To promote psychosocial wellness and trauma healing.
• To reduce crime, drug use, and early pregnancies in the community.

Target Group

• Vulnerable youth (both boys and girls)
• Young mothers
• Street-connected youth
• Youth in conflict with the law
• School dropouts
